Computer Vision: Running
Tutorial By Ferdz Duterte
Introduction

Running gait assessment is critical in improving performance and injury prevention. On top of that, gait assessments also help diagnose certain pathologies or assess people’s health and recovery. Traditional approaches to running gait assessment are often constrained by unnatural running environments such as indoor labs. The experience is also very subjective, and the cost of equipment for such an assessment is very high.

Use of wearables devices (smartwatches and smart rings) have shown promise but despite their flexible use in any environment and reduced cost, they still face issues with connectivity to mobile platforms and inaccurate measurements.

With that, development of valid, reproducible and low-cost approaches are key. Simple vision-based systems have a considerable potential in this area, as they allow the capture of gait in unconstrained environments, such as at home or in a clinic, while the required computations can be done remotely.

In this tutorial, we will explore a non-wearable camera-based approach to running gait assessment, focused on identification of initial contact events (the moment when the foot touches the ground) within a runner’s stride.
